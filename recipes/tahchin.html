Tahchin

Tahchin is a traditional Persian rice dish made with saffron-infused rice, yogurt, and eggs, often layered with chicken or lamb.
This recipe focuses on the chicken version but also includes a vegetarian variation featuring stinging nettles.
It is layered and baked until there is a golden, crispy crust on the outside called tahdig.
The dish is usually served with barberries, pistachios, or almonds but I don't decorate it. Flipping it is spectacular enough.

Ingredients & Instructions

Outer Crust (same for both versions)

  • 3 large eggs - yolks only
  • 2 cups plain yogurt (Make sure it’s tangy yogurt, Astro Balkan is good if you need a suggestion)
  • 2 Tbsp neutral oil
  • ½ tsp saffron threads, bloomed in 2 Tbsp hot water
  • Salt – ¾ Tbsp per cup of rice (so ~1½ Tbsp total)
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups rice (parboiled basmati or long-grain rice, partially cooked, needs to be slightly firm in the center aka al dente)
  1. In a large bowl, mix together egg yolks, yogurt, oil, bloomed saffron, salt, and pepper.
  2. Stir in the parboiled rice until well coated.
  3. Set aside.

Barberries (same for both versions)

  • ½ cup dried barberries
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 Tbsp bloomed saffron (optional, extra color & aroma)
  1. In a small pan, heat up some oil and add the barberries. Sauté for 1 minute, then add butter and optional saffron.
  2. Stir until the barberries get plump then set aside.

Chicken Filling

  • 1 lb (450 g) chicken thighs or breast, cut into chunks
  • 1-2 medium carrots, cut into pieces
  • 3–4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp mustard powder or 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt & pepper
  • Water (enough to just cover chicken)
  1. In a pot, place the chicken with carrots, garlic, tomato paste, turmeric, smoked paprika, cumin, mustard, salt, and pepper. Add enough water to just cover the chicken.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until chicken is cooked through, about 20-25 minutes.
  3. Remove chicken from the broth and shred or cut into smaller pieces. Set aside.
    4. In a pot, combine chicken, carrot, garlic, spices, and enough water to cover.

Stinging Nettle Filling

  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups nettles (or spinach, kale, or chard), roughly chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp cumin
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt & pepper
  • 2 Tbsp oil
  • 2 Tbsp barberries
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 Tbsp bloomed saffron (optional)
  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add turmeric, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add chopped nettles and cook until nettles are wilted and tender.
  4. Mix barberries into the nettle-onion mixture; stir on low heat for 2–3 minutes. Set aside.

Assembly & Baking (both versions)

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Grease a deep 9-inch (23 cm) round baking dish or springform pan with oil or butter.
  3. Spread half of the rice mixture evenly at the bottom of the dish, pressing down firmly to create a compact layer.
  4. Add the chicken filling (or nettle filling for vegetarian version) on top of the rice layer, spreading it out evenly.
  5. Top with the remaining rice mixture, pressing down firmly again.
  6. Cover the dish tightly with aluminum foil.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 1 to 1.5 hours.
  8. Remove from oven and let it cool for 10-15 minutes before flipping onto a serving plate.
  9. Garnish with sautéed barberries and serve warm.
